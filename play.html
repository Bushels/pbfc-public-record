Act A · How a clean feeder association works

The machine, before it breaks

Thirty seconds of rules. You’ll need them when the dirt shows up.

1. Who can join Members must actually farm on Alberta land they own or lease — not pure paper investors. Primary purpose: grow/finish livestock under the program.
FAGR s.11
2. What the money can buy Guaranteed loan proceeds: livestock for members, allowed advances, approved projects, price insurance, related admin costs. Not free-form spending.
FAGA s.4 · FAGR s.13
3. Who owns the cattle On a purchase loan, livestock stays association property (branded/tagged) until the member pays out. Sale proceeds go to the co-op first.
Membership agreement pattern · Affidavit
4. The 5% vault Before supply or advance: collect ≥5% of total monetary obligation (ex interest) into a separate security deposit account.
FAGR s.33
5. Caps & equity advances Roughly $3M total monetary obligation caps (ex advances). Equity advances limited (often 50% of price-to-FMV gain unless insured/forward contracted).
FAGR s.30 · s.31
6. Paper & inspections Keep real sales documents. Don’t obstruct Ministry inspectors. Board holds meetings; conflicts and compensation stay under control.
FAGR s.17 · FAGA s.9 · Order V7–V10
