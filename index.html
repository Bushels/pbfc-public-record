What happened

Four layers of truth. We keep them separate on purpose.

Ministry Court RO reports

Picture Butte Feeder Cooperative (PBFC) is an Alberta cooperative that finances cattle for members through the Feeder Associations Loan Guarantee (FALG) program. A bank syndicate lends to PBFC; the Province guarantees a slice; members get purchase loans and equity advances against livestock.

After inspections from September 2023 to May 2024, the Ministry prepared a report dated November 18, 2024. On January 27/28, 2025, Minister RJ Sigurdson ordered a freeze on further guaranteed advances, citing noncompliance with FAGA/FAGR and “unsound” operations. The board, administrator, and supervisor exited. On February 21, 2025 the Court of King’s Bench appointed Alvarez & Marsal as Restructuring Officer (Action 2501-02733).

Why this site exists

Most of the story is already in public filings — if you know where to look. This archive compresses that paper into a navigable record with page-level citations, and treats missing documents as first-class objects rather than rumors.

Epistemic rule: Ministry findings are opinions after inspection — not criminal convictions. Court orders approve process and fees. RO reports describe remediation. Contested claims stay labeled.

The Order findings (V1–V10)

Source: Appendix to the Minister’s Order, reproduced as Exhibit “A” to the Affidavit of Tony Ankermann (sworn February 18, 2025). These are the Minister’s opinions after reviewing the Inspection Report.

Report recovered: the detailed facts behind these categories are in the November 18, 2024 IIS Inspection Report, embedded as Confidential Exhibit “A” to the filed unredacted Ankermann affidavit. They remain IIS findings and opinions—some significantly disputed—not criminal convictions.
V1

Ineligible feeder members

Accepted members who did not meet land/farming requirements; memberships not primarily for growing/finishing livestock.

FAGR s.11
V2

Improper use of guaranteed loan proceeds

Proceeds used outside allowed purposes — including transactions for members who had withdrawn.

FAGA s.4 · FAGR s.13
V3

Supplying livestock / advances despite contravention

Order frames ineligibility; statute text also covers default, supervision, and Provincial Supervisor orders.

FAGR s.14(3)
V4

Advances above allowed equity percentage

Advances exceeding 50% of the difference between purchase price and fair market value (subject to insurance/forward-contract exceptions).

FAGR s.30(2)
V5

Exceeding total monetary obligation caps

Livestock supplied above prescribed member limits (generally $3M excluding advances).

FAGR s.31(1)
V6

Security deposits not properly collected

Failed to collect the required ≥5% security deposits from certain members.

FAGR s.33
V7

Obstructing inspections

Staff refused interviews; NDAs; Chairman Schooten interference with a witness interview; unanswered questions about amended board minutes.

FAGA s.9(3)
V8

Inadequate sales documents

Missing purchaser names, prices, and failure to provide records on request.

FAGR s.17
V9

Unsound practices — administration

POAs, irregular documents/signatures, equity advances used to fund deposits, processing withdrawn members, weak minutes and financial records.

FAGA s.6(1)(b)
V10

Unsound practices — board

Eligibility failures, related transactions, conflicts of interest, excessive board compensation/expenses, AGM not held timely.

FAGA s.6(1)(b)

Ankermann’s affidavit notes that certain findings were “the subject of significant dispute by certain parties.” Sealing of the Confidential Affidavit was sought; Justice Mah denied sealing (2025 ABKB 162).

Rebuild under the Restructuring Officer

After the freeze: inventory, credit docs, new staff, new bylaws, new board — then a Ministry “sound manner” letter.

What the RO says was fixed

  • Cattle audit reconciled inventory (Mar 2025)
  • Order rescinded May 1, 2025; loans May 26
  • New Supervisor / Administrator hired
  • Amended bylaws & policies Court-approved Sept 2025 (conflicts, volunteer board, no equity-funded deposits)
  • Board election Dec 1, 2025; AGM Feb 25, 2026
  • MNP unqualified FY2025 audit (Jan 19, 2026)
  • Ministry Apr 23, 2026: operating in a sound manner

What remediation is not

Later compliance and a clean audit do not automatically mean the historical findings were false, overstated, or fully tested in public. The recovered Inspection Report describes transaction samples, member files and interviews; many underlying records it cites are not embedded in that exhibit and have not been independently reviewed here. Those supporting records remain an important gap.

Remediation ≠ acquittal Court ≠ AGM Opinion ≠ conviction

Costs, fees, and the AGM claim

People sometimes say “the AGM validated all the costs.” The filings support a more careful sentence.

What the AGM did (Feb 25, 2026)

Per the RO Third Report: presented FY2025 audited financial statements, operational/RO updates, appointed MNP for FY2026. Attendance was limited (21 registered members / 12.7%). It is not described as a forensic clearance of V1–V10 or a ratification that pre-crisis practices were proper.

What the Court did

Court orders approved Restructuring Officer conduct and professional fees in stages (June 30 and Sept 17, 2025 hearings; further fees sought April 2026). That is taxation of restructuring work — not a blessing of the original Ministry findings.
